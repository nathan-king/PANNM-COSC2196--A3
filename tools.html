Communication Tools

MS Teams

After forming our group on the Canvas Discussion board, MS Teams has been the primary communication form. We are utilising the built-in discussion board to post about essential communications, using the Teams Meeting site to have our catchups and the file-sharing for agendas, actions, and sharing of other documents relevant to this assignment. However, the tool we found essential was the Chat function on MS Teams used for general ad-lib discussions to share ideas, reminders, or check-in. With the team split between the West Coast and East Coast of Australia, a tool such as this for ad-hoc discussions has proved extremely useful. Copies of the Agendas, recordings of Meetings and Actions evolving from those meetings available on the below links.

GitHub

PPANNM has utilised GitHub as the tool of choice to build the WebSite to support our presentation and project for this assessment. The team are very proud of the skill sets they have developed on this journey together. With a diverse background, our team members offer a range of complementary skillsets to help the rest of the team establish their skills. Our team only had one member who had previously done any website building and experience with GitHub, while others had stronger report writing and research experience.
With the team supporting each other, all have improved their skills. It was amazing to see the website we all produced for Assessment 2. Now what we have created for Assessment 3 and early developments of artefacts for our project USE ME NOW application. When used correctly with good detail attached to each commit, the audit trail allows us as a team to follow the path of what each member has been completing and updating on the website, working collaboratively to produce a professional finish that we are all are incredibly proud of what we have created.
