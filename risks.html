Risks

We have identified several risks associated with this project. Those specific to this project have been put into three categories: Technical, Operational and External.

Technical:

  • Too many users – Online functions fail (Stangarone 2013)
    Online features can fail to respond when hit by a spike of traffic. This traffic can be caused maliciously, by bad actors targeting a specific service with a DDOS (Distributed Denial of Service) attack, or unintentionally due to unexpected demand. Our project can be vulnerable to this due to its reliance on online features, such as the Open Food Facts Database referenced in Tools & Technologies.
  • Product failure – it doesn’t work on a certain model of phone (or all phones)
    With the numerous available devices in the market, particularly on the Android side, there is a risk of running into a hardware/software combination that isn’t compatible with our app. This can be mitigated by closely following design conventions for the respective operating systems. Testing on several different phones is also essential to make sure that the app operates and appears the same across various devices.
  • Database compromised with inaccurate entries
    Without any validation and relying on open source databases, it is possible for corruption to creep into the database due to deliberate false entries or errors made when submitting information. This can be mitigated with validation and manual review, however manual reviewing would add to the cost of operating the project.
