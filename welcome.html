Group Process

Overall we believe our group worked great together. We all brought our own set of skills and knowledge, which allowed us to compliment one and others strengths and identify weaknesses. Using TEAMs, our communication lines were always open, everyone remained accountable, and no one was afraid to ask for help. We met three times a week throughout assignment two for video chats to go over our workload. However, we have now dropped this down to two meetings per week, as we have built a strong communication network and are aware of our varying work and study schedules.

Nathan King | Senior .Net Developer

To become a .NET developer, there are numerous career paths one might undertake. One such pathway is formal education through certification in programming, a certification in information technology with an emphasis on programming, or a computer science certification. Another path is through self-study and the undertaking of informal courses. Regardless of the pathway chosen, it is required that one become proficient in at least one .NET language, such as C#, F# and Visual Basic. Secondly, it is necessary to have a firm base of knowledge in programming concepts, algorithms, design patterns, databases and data structures. Finally, as ASP.NET makes up a large part of the .NET framework, a deep understanding of server and client-size web development is usually necessary, along with JavaScript, the dominant programming language for web development its frameworks provide a considerable advantage.

My plan towards becoming a .NET developer started in self-study on developing web applications in JavaScript using React. I have since started a Bachelor of Information Technology, where I intend to learn about software development on a profound level. While I do not expect to learn a lot, specifically on .NET, I will harness the knowledge garnered throughout my studies and my understanding of web development to precipitate my learning of C# and .NET development. Furthermore, I intend to start building applications using C#/.NET and creating connections within the .NET community throughout my degree.

I am undertaking a Bachelor of Business (Financial Planning) on the approved list of degrees (FASEA Approved Courses by Higher Education Providers 2020). Therefore, upon completion of this degree, in March 2022, I can undertake my professional year. As I am already in the industry as I paraplanner, I can transition to Financial Adviser's role and complete my professional year with my current company. The professional year comprises four quarters with the FASEA Exam conducted before the 3rd Quarter(“The Financial Adviser Examination | FASEA” 2020). Therefore by mid-2023, I could be a fully accredited Financial Adviser.
